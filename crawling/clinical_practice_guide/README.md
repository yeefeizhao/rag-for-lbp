# Clinical Practice Guidelines

This directory contains clinical practice guideline (CPG) documents for lower back pain management from leading healthcare organizations and professional societies.

## Overview

Clinical practice guidelines provide evidence-based recommendations for the diagnosis, treatment, and management of lower back pain. These guidelines synthesize current research and expert consensus to inform clinical decision-making.

## Guidelines Included

### `academy_of_orthopaedic_physical_therapy.pdf`
Guidelines from the Academy of Orthopaedic Physical Therapy (AOPT), a component of the American Physical Therapy Association (APTA). Focuses on physical therapy interventions and rehabilitation protocols for lower back pain.

### `american_associations_of_family_physicians.pdf`
Clinical practice recommendations from the American Association of Family Physicians (AAFP) for primary care management of lower back pain in community settings.

### `american_college_of_physicians.pdf`
Evidence-based guidelines from the American College of Physicians (ACP) covering diagnostic approaches and treatment recommendations for acute and chronic lower back pain.

### `australia.pdf`
Australian national guidelines for lower back pain management, reflecting best practices and healthcare system considerations specific to Australia.

### `canada.pdf`
Canadian clinical practice guidelines for lower back pain, incorporating evidence from Canadian healthcare research and tailored to the Canadian healthcare context.

### `kaiser_permanente.pdf`
Kaiser Permanente's integrated care approach to lower back pain management, emphasizing coordinated care and evidence-based treatment pathways.

### `north_american_spine_society.pdf`
Comprehensive guidelines from the North American Spine Society (NASS), a multidisciplinary medical society focused on spine care, covering both conservative and surgical management options.

### `review_of_cpgs.pdf`
A systematic review or meta-analysis of multiple clinical practice guidelines, providing comparative analysis and synthesis of recommendations across different guideline sources.

### `va-department_of_defense.pdf`
Joint clinical practice guidelines from the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) and Department of Defense (DoD) for the management of lower back pain in military and veteran populations.

### `world_health_organization.pdf`
World Health Organization (WHO) guidelines on lower back pain management, providing a global perspective and considerations for different healthcare resource settings.

## Usage

These PDF documents are:
1. **Extracted** by the `scrape.py` script using PyPDF2
2. **Converted** to XML format for storage
3. **Processed** for inclusion in the RAG system's knowledge base
4. **Used** to generate evidence-based responses to patient queries

## Key Topics Covered

- Diagnostic criteria and clinical assessment
- Non-pharmacological interventions (physical therapy, exercise, manipulation)
- Pharmacological management (NSAIDs, muscle relaxants, opioid guidelines)
- Imaging recommendations (when X-ray, MRI, or CT is appropriate)
- Red flag symptoms requiring immediate attention
- Chronic pain management strategies
- Patient education and self-management
- Return-to-work considerations
- Interdisciplinary care coordination
